Things Read

Altered Carbon: Richard K. Morgan; SF
In the future your brain can be downloaded into new bodies; a man who committed suicide is sure he was murdered.
Broken Angels: Richard K. Morgan; SF
In the same brain downloading future, a man tries to avoid a war by digging up a spaceship.
Storm Front: Jim Butcher, Fantasy
The first Harry Dresden book; recommended by Kitt. Does a good job world building.
The Cold Dish: Craig Johnson, Mystery
The first Walt Longmire book; also recommended by Kitt. Some parts were hard to follow because I don't know American native history, but fun to read. Very twisty.
War Dogs: Greg Bear, SF
Space marines on mars. There's a lot of winding around before the plot gets to the point.
The Circle: Dave Eggers
An unnerving take on what working at a Big Internet Companyâ„¢ might be like in the future.
The Shell Collector: Hugh Howey
A neat globe-trotting story about high-value shell collecting.
The Hard Thing About Hard Things: Ben Horowitz, Business
Ben Horowitz tells a lot of stories about how he repeatedly pulled the fat out of the fire, and how you will need to do that too some day. Has a good section about one on ones.
Woken Furies: Richard K. Morgan; SF
A story develops from a grudge and a bar fight, and progresses from there. Lots of sci fi action.
The Icarus Deception: Seth Godin; Business
A focused rallying cry to Make More Art.
Hollow World: Michael J Sullivan; Thriller
A man inadverently travels farther into the future than he means to, ending up as the last man on earth.
Good to Great: Jim Collins; Business
The published results of a study on how companies go from being good, bad, or mediocre to great - lots of fascinating stories about each of the companies studied (and the ones they were compared to that didn't do as well).
Payments Systems in the U.S.
A pretty neat deep dive into all the different ways that money moves around in the United States.
Fall of the Core: Netcast 01
A short novella that's told almost entirely from the perspective of a newscaster covering the end of the world. Not as good as I was hoping.
The Better Man Project
Basically a condensed collection of the tips from Men's Health, grouped into chapters by focus. Some useful things, but mostly - eat more produce and exercise more.
The End of All Things: John Scalzi; Military SF
I really enjoyed these four interwoven novellas; I haven't read Old Man's War yet, but after reading these I am definitely going to have to check it out.

Games Played

Alto's Adventure
A cute infinite runner about snowboarding and llamas. Really well done goal progression; reminiscent of Tiny Wings.
Strike Suit Zero
You are a space pilot. You gain control of a fighter that can transform into a mech. Weird concept, but they nailed space opera game controls. Firing a missile never felt so good.
Battlestar Galactica
A game of deceipt and secret strategies. Takes a while (2hrs+) to play; can end friendships.
MUJO
A match-three puzzler game with an interesting stacking mechanic and an energy gate that feels kind of forced.
Taichi Panda
A korean-style iOS MMORPG. Good combat; reminds me a lot of Diablo 3.
Tokaido
A game about accumulating the most life experience while crossing Japan's east sea road. Probably the most peaceful, quietly competitive game I've played.
Dungeon of the Endless
This game is a mashup of a lot of different concepts - roguelike procedural exploration, squad tactics, and tower defense. It's also turned based, which was a surprise. Pretty good; I found singleplayer to be crushingly difficult, but hear multiplayer is better.
Astro Duel
A neat little space dogfighting game.
Lifeline...
A really well delivered game about helping a stranded astronaut named Taylor, who you communicate with via pseudo-SMS only.
Gomo
I bought this game for the art, but got stuck on the puzzles.
Royals
Steep learning curve, but rewarding to play. I'm really impressed with how solid the UI is, especially when dealing with followers vs. yourself.
Cook, Serve, Delicious!
A restaurant simulation game with pretty well done controls, if a slightly simple tutorial (wall of text).
Burrito Bison
A pretty good launch game; the variety in the gummies you encounter and the fact that you have to do more than just watch the game play itself is nice. I also thought it was cool that it has an end.
(ASMR) Vin Diesel DMing a Game of D&D Just For You
I was surprised by how relaxing this was to play, and a little sad when it was over.
Sproggiwood
A really well done turn-based roguelike with an interesting inventory/city building mechanic that makes finding gear much more rewarding. I have high hopes that Rogue Wizards will be like this.
Ninja Pizza Girl
I got access to an early build of this - the game has potential, but it's not there yet.
Clicker Heroes
A master class in skinner box design, packaged in an incremental game. Has an interesting reset-but-play-through-faster mechanic.
Rayman Legends
I read a review of this game that called it "platforming perfection"; I'm inclined to agree. Super well done and the co-op is fantastic as well (and fully co-operative; nothing you do detracts from the other player in any way, and you can't steal their resources).
Tsuro
A game about building interconnected paths in order to knock out the other dragons. Super easy to get into.
The Resistance
Another lying game like Battlestar Galactica, but heavier on the lying and much less complex. Good, although hard for the good guys to win (which makes those ones feel even more rewarding).
Flash Point: Fire Rescue
You and friends play firefighters trying to get people out of a burning building. This game had a lot of complexity to it that none of us were expecting; we lost because I don't think we understood what to do for the first half of the game.
Exploding Kittens
Reminds me of UNO a little bit - the start of the game is kind of slow, but the intensity continually escalates. The end of the game becomes very high intensity as everyone tries to take each other out.
Quarto!
A connect four game that's based on recognizing the similarities between the pieces you're connecting, with an interesting mechanic: the other player chooses what piece you place next.
Mr. Jack
A stealth game where one player plays Jack the ripper and the other plays a detective trying to catch him. Interesting mechanics, although a bit slow to get started.
Firefly: The Game
Very cool movement mechanic where you can mosey along or go for full engine burns, but on our first playthrough we struggled with figuring out whether we wanted to play the game for a while and experience it all or just race to the goal. Comes with multiple scenarios so that the victory condition is different each time you play, which opens up a lot of options for playing it again but being forced into different strategies. A little complex at first.
Super Mario Maker
If you ever wanted to create your own Mario levels, this is the game for you. Lots of interesting interactions between game pieces, a solid level editor, and more levels and creativity on display than you can shake a stick at.
